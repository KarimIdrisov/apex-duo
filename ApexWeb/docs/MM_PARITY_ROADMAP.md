# Motorsport Manager parity — roadmap (Apex Web)

> Generated 2026-06-21 from a 19-agent gap analysis (research MM × map ApexWeb × adversarial verify × synthesize). Packaging decision (owner): **in-place** — deepen ApexWeb toward MM richness, keep the deliberate divergences, add a lightweight lobby ruleset/preset layer for MM-selectable variants. Execution: autonomous, phase by phase, each shippable & verified (node --test + tools/balance.mjs).

## Parity

~52% of MM core+major mechanics already covered (of ~182 classified mechanics: ~54 full 'have', ~58 'partial', ~55 'missing', ~22 deliberate 'divergent'; scoring have=1 + partial=0.5 over the ~160 non-by-design-scope mechanics gives ~83/160 ≈ 52%). Critically, the coverage is front-loaded onto the CORE spine — the entire live race-weekend loop, the strategy sim, the season meta-loop, and the driver layer are 'have' — so the felt parity is higher than the raw percentage; the missing 48% is concentrated in depth (red-zone parts, component tiers, staff/HQ buildings, binding finance) and two XL structural departures (multi-series ladder, GMA voting) that are partly intentional scope.

ApexWeb already implements the entire MM spine: a live three-session race weekend (practice + setup + knockout quali + real-time race with pause/speed), a deep tyre/fuel/pace/order/pit strategy sim, a season-over-season meta loop (calendar -> board confidence -> dev -> off-season reset/aging/academy), a continuous car-development model with risk/reliability trade-offs, a full driver attribute/morale/contract/scouting layer, and a finance/sponsor/board economy. Across ~182 classified mechanics roughly 54 are full 'have', 58 'partial', 55 'missing' and 22 deliberate 'divergent'. The biggest MM-feel gaps are NOT the weekend loop (which is largely done) but four clusters: (1) in-race depth MM has and ApexWeb lacks — ERS/energy, in-race part wear + red-zone + repairs, two-sided tyre temperature, 5-step driving styles, player fuel-load and team orders; (2) the development progression puzzle — Known Components/tiers, Improvability, pre-season chassis-supplier ritual and per-part designer stats; (3) the staff/HQ depth — per-car mechanics, mechanic perks, the R&D building tree (Wind Tunnel/Simulator/Staff Centre), and staff potential/age; (4) the management meta — insolvency-as-real-constraint, marketability->sponsor economy, mid-season ultimatum, and (XL) the multi-series ladder + GMA voting. ApexWeb is so MM-shaped already that 'parity' is mostly deepening existing systems, not bolting on a new game.

## Packaging: in-place

ApexWeb is already ~MM-shaped and mature (deterministic sim, 60 ES modules, 401 passing tests) and the gap analysis shows the spine is DONE — every divergence the team chose (continuous tyre wear, level+ceiling parts, flat F1 economy, 13-attr driver taxonomy, season-based contracts) is a deliberate, documented design improvement, not an accident to be reverted. A separate-fork would duplicate ~52% of the work that is already correct and would split the 401-test safety net; an mm-mode-flag would force every system (tyre model, parts model, economy, attribute taxonomy) to carry two parallel implementations behind toggles, doubling sim complexity for a game whose core math is hand-tuned and deterministic. The honest reading of 'make it play like MM' is: deepen the systems that are partial/missing toward MM richness while KEEPING the deliberate divergences. The few places where MM and ApexWeb genuinely conflict (banded vs continuous tyre life; discrete part inventory vs level+ceiling; multi-series ladder vs single F1 series) are exactly the divergences to preserve, so there is little to fork over. Build in-place; only introduce a lightweight ruleset/preset layer (lobby options) where MM offers player-selectable variants (SC/VSC regime, start type, points table) so 'MM-faithful' becomes a set of selectable rules rather than a forked codebase. If multi-series + GMA voting (the two true XL structural departures) are ever pursued, gate THOSE behind a 'career mode' selector — but that is a future expansion, not a fork of the existing game.

## Phases

### Phase 1: Phase 1 — In-race feel: the levers MM players reach for  — effort L

**Goal.** Close the highest-visibility in-race gaps so a minute of ApexWeb racing feels like MM: more effort steps, two-sided tyre heat, player fuel control, the start-tyre rule, and team orders for the two co-op cars.

**Why here.** These are the cheapest-per-MM-feel wins: the weekend loop is already faithful, so the felt difference between ApexWeb and MM is concentrated in moment-to-moment levers (effort steps, heat, fuel, team orders). All are extensions of existing, well-tested systems (S/M effort each) rather than new subsystems, so they ship fast and immediately move the 'plays like MM' needle. Team orders specifically matter because ApexWeb is two-car co-op — a notable current omission.

### Phase 2: Phase 2 — In-race part wear, red zone & repairs  — effort XL

**Why here.** This is the single largest MISSING core in-race system (flagged core/XL in two domains). It depends on nothing from Phase 1 but is heavier, so it follows the quick wins. It unlocks three dependent MM mechanics at once (red-zone gambling, in-race repairs, pit sequencing) and finally gives reliability a felt in-race consequence instead of a hidden dice roll — a defining MM tension.

## Deliberate divergences — KEEP (do not regress toward MM)

- [dev] Continuous tyre-life wear curve with an accelerating cliff instead of MM's discrete High/Medium/Low banded life — explicitly validated (LOGIC_AUDIT §19) as what makes stint length and the undercut matter; keep the model, only sharpen the cliff and add a precise lap-window HUD (don't reintroduce bands)
- [dev] Level + ceiling part-development model instead of MM's discrete bolt-on part inventory (masterplan §13) — add MM's progression texture (tiers/improvability/supplier ritual) ON TOP, but never adopt discrete per-copy part stock, replica-build costs, or two-best-per-category carry tactics
- [scope] Single F1 2026 series with a normalised 0..1.20 indicator scale instead of MM's 7-series/3-category ladder and per-championship Min/Max stat ranges — keep the single-series focus; multi-series + promotion/relegation is a future opt-in expansion, not a regression target
- [driver] 13-attribute taxonomy (pace+quali+composure+discipline+smoothness etc.) instead of MM's cornering/braking/control trio — the collapsed pace + split composure/discipline model is intentional; do NOT split pace back into three braking/cornering/control bars
- [finance] Flat ~2.3x F1-realistic prize spread (deliberately anti-runaway, FINANCE doc §3) instead of MM's top-heavy ~$500m/$250m/$125m tier pools — keep the field-compressing economy; add selectable merit-distribution presets as an OPTION, don't default to top-heavy
- [meta] Co-director specialty perks (aero/engine/strategist/mechanic/financier/mentor) built around two-player co-op instead of MM's solo manager-background/Politico perks — this is a deliberate co-op-first reframing; keep it
- [dev] Engine-supply modelled as backer archetype (works vs customer) + multi-season PU program instead of MM's annual 4-category supplier re-pick — keep the archetype/program spine; the Phase-4 pre-season chassis-supplier picks should layer onto it, not replace the works/customer economy
- [race] Three live practice sessions (P1/P2/P3) on an accelerated game clock instead of MM's single Friday session — the F1-Manager-style three-session loop is richer; keep it
- [meta] Automatic cyclic regulation 'eras' that reshuffle part weightings instead of MM's GMA rule-voting — keep the era system as the default 'rules shift year to year' feel; GMA voting, if ever added, is an additive layer, not a replacement
- [dev] One-stage Design pipeline (design -> auto-install next weekend) instead of MM's Design->Build->Fit with a separate build queue and Fit-Parts screen (masterplan §5.2) — the collapsed pipeline is a deliberate UX simplification; keep it
